## Currently
Co-founder of Mountain Futures GmbH, a WSL/SLF spin-off providing science-based consulting on mountain water resources, cryosphere modelling, and climate risk. Nearly two decades of experience spanning cutting-edge research, operational tool development, and applied project delivery for SDC, WMO, World Bank, and the UN system. Lead developer of widely adopted open-source tools for climate downscaling and snow modelling. Affiliated researcher at the [WSL Institute for Snow and Avalanche Research](http://www.slf.ch/) in Davos, Switzerland.

## Employment & Projects

`current`
__Mountain Futures GmbH (since 2025)__ _Co-Founder_. WSL/SLF spin-off providing science-based consulting on mountain water resources, cryosphere monitoring, and climate risk. Clients include multilateral development banks, bilateral donors, and international organisations operating in High Mountain Asia.

`2022-present`
__SDC SAPPHIRE__ _Technical Lead, Snow & Cryosphere_. Technical lead for snow and cryosphere within the SDC SAPPHIRE Central Asia programme (Smart & Precise Prognostic Hydrology in Central Asia). Developed and operates the SnowMapper — an operational 500m resolution snow monitoring and forecasting system covering five Central Asian countries, benchmarked against a 24-year climatology. The system is built on the <a href="https://doi.org/10.21105/joss.05059">TopoPyScale</a> toolchain.

`2023-present`
__World Meteorological Organization__ _Contributing Author_. Annual snow assessment for the WMO Global Cryosphere Watch programme, providing the Central Asia regional analysis. <a href="https://globalcryospherewatch.org/assessments/snow/">GCW Snow Assessments</a>

`2021-25`
__WSL Institute for Snow and Avalanche Research SLF__ _Technical Lead, SDC Projects_. Co-PI and technical lead on two SDC-funded programmes totalling CHF 1.3M+: <a href="https://www.eda.admin.ch/deza/de/home/themen/wasser.html/content/dezaprojects/SDC/en/2019/7F10279/phase1?oldPagePath=/content/deza/de/home/themen/wasser.html">CROMO-ADAPT</a> (cryosphere monitoring and modelling, Central Asia) and <a href="https://www.caritas.ch/en/what-we-do/worldwide/climate/tajikistan-weather-data-helps-farmers-to-improve-their-productivity.html">WWCS</a> (weather and climate services, Tajikistan, with CARITAS CH). Delivered operational tools, climate assessments, and training for national hydromet services across the region. Related publications include regional cryosphere assessments (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.wasec.2020.100072">Barandun et al. 2020</a>) and glacier mass balance modelling (<a href="https://doi.org/10.5194/tc-16-5001-2022">Kronenberg et al. 2022</a>).

`2018-25`
__WSL Institute for Snow and Avalanche Research SLF__ _Research Scientist_. Research on snow processes in High Mountain Asia using numerical models, EO data, and field campaigns. SNSF project "From Cloud to Ground: Snow Accumulation in Extreme Environments". Contributing scientist, Swiss Polar Institute <a href="https://pamir-project.ch/">PAMIR</a> flagship project. WMO <a href="https://public.wmo.int/en/our-mandate/focus-areas/cryosphere/GCW">Global Cryosphere Watch</a> expert. Research outputs include studies on snow surface energy exchange (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1007/s10546-024-00864-y">González-Herrero et al. 2024</a>), high alpine climate change (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3389/feart.2020.00100">Bender et al. 2020</a>), and downscaling intercomparison (<a href="https://doi.org/10.3389/feart.2022.789332">Kruyt et al. 2022</a>).

`2024-25`
__UNESCO/Global Environment Facility__ _Consultant_. Developed high-resolution climate change impact scenarios for snow cover across Central Asia under a GEF/UNDP/UNESCO regional project strengthening cryosphere monitoring and climate resilience in five countries. Outputs contributed to national and regional strategic action programmes for climate adaptation.

`2025-26`
__Landell Mills Ltd (EU)__ _Hydrological Modeller_. Revival of Balochistan Water Resources Programme (RBWRP) — climate downscaling and hydrological modelling across 214 catchments in Balochistan, Pakistan. Built an interactive <a href="https://joelfiddes-bwrp.share.connect.posit.cloud/">climate and hydrology dashboard</a> for exploring current conditions and future scenarios (SSP1-2.6, SSP2-4.5, SSP5-8.5). EU-funded programme implemented with FAO.

`2023-24`
__FutureWater__ _Consultant_. Project development and partner coordination for integrated water resources management in Tajikistan. <a href="https://www.futurewater.eu/projects/integrated-water-resources-management-in-tajikistan/">FutureWater project page</a>.

`2022`
__World Meteorological Organization__ _Consultant_. Inventory and gap analysis of mountain cryosphere observation networks across five Central Asian countries. Delivered recommendations for strengthening regional hydromet capacity in line with WMO standards. Published as <a href="https://library.wmo.int/records/item/69490">WMO GCW Report No. 1</a>.

`2021-23`
__FutureWater__ _Consultant_. Glacio-hydrological modelling and IWRM planning for the Bhagirathi sub-basin, Uttarakhand, India. Developed present-day and future hydrological scenarios to support water allocation and climate adaptation policy under SDC's SCA-Himalayas programme. Co-authored guidelines for glacio-hydrological modelling in High Mountain Asia. <a href="https://www.futurewater.eu/projects/development-of-a-glacio-hydrological-model-and-iwrm-plan-for-the-uttarakhand-subbasin-in-india/">FutureWater project page</a>.

__World Bank__ _Consultant_. Forensic assessment of the May 2021 disasters in Tajikistan (World Bank SCINHP, P158298). Analysed cryospheric and hydrometeorological contributing factors using remote sensing and modelling. Findings delivered to the World Bank and Government of Tajikistan. Published as <a href="https://www.preventionweb.net/publication/assessment-contributing-factors-may-2021-disasters-tajikistan-forensic-study-under">Allen et al. 2021</a>.

__Permian Global__ _Consultant_. Developed automated remote sensing tools to estimate deforestation rates and support carbon stock assessments for conservation finance projects in tropical forests. Contributing to research published in <a href="https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aay4490">Philipson et al. 2020, Science</a>.

`2018-20`

Developed a data assimilation and uncertainty quantification framework for large-area snowpack simulations in mountain regions, integrating satellite retrievals with physically-based snow models. Work resulted in the hyper-resolution ensemble snow reanalysis method published in <a href="https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-23-4717-2019">HESS (2019)</a>.

`2017-18`
__World Meteorological Organization__ _Consultant_. Developed data interoperability tools for the <a href="https://joelfiddes.github.io/wmo_gcw/">WMO Global Cryosphere Watch</a> programme, enabling standardised exchange between CryoNet monitoring stations and the GCW data portal. Methodology published in <a href="http://doi.org/10.5334/dsj-2020-006">Bavay et al. 2020</a>.

`2014-17`
__WSL Institute for Snow and Avalanche Research SLF__ _Data Scientist_. Developed and managed environmental data acquisition and management systems for the SwissEx/OSPER project, a large interdisciplinary monitoring initiative with multiple ETH domain partners.

`2016`
__Himalaya Permafrost ETH SEED__ _Scientist_. Establishment of high altitude permafrost monitoring sites in Langtang, Nepal. Training young Nepalese scientists. International permafrost and data tools workshop with participants from Afghanistan, India and Nepal. Published in <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Simon_Allen3/publication/305999744_Permafrost_studies_in_Kullu_district_Himachal_Pradesh/links/57a9b15708aef300152a09a4.pdf">Allen et al. 2016</a>.

__University of Zurich__ _Scientist_. Managing PERMOS network of ground and rock temperature measurements at Jungfraujoch, Schilthorn, and Piz Corvatsch, including extensive fieldwork and rope-access work. Processing GST, meteorological station, and borehole data.

`2014`
__University of Zurich__ _Scientist_. Swiss Development Cooperation/DEZA funded climate change adaptation programme. Developing baseline maps for key land surface variables in the Indian Himalaya in coordination with several Indian government agencies, led by the Department of Science & Technology. Published in <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Simon_Allen3/publication/305999744_Permafrost_studies_in_Kullu_district_Himachal_Pradesh/links/57a9b15708aef300152a09a4.pdf">Allen et al. 2016</a>.

`2009-13`
__University of Zurich__ _Scientific Researcher / PhD Candidate_. Three first-author publications, oral and poster presentations at international conferences. Supervision of MSc and BSc students. Fieldwork in high mountain environments.
