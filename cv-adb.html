12. Detailed Tasks Assigned

Climate specialist/hydrologist (3.0 person-months). The specialist will have a post-graduate degree in hydrology or related area; a minimum of 15 years of experience working directly with large hydro-meteorological datasets; and at least 10 years of experience using remote-sensing data, undertaking assessments of climate change impacts and climate adaptation, and using hydrometeorological data in operational information services, e.g., early warning. The specialist will be familiar with the hydrology and climate impacts in Central Asia.

Early warning systems and climate variability:

  • Assess trends of increasing climate variability and recommend how regional and national water management agencies can more effectively monitor variability and build information sharing into operational management at pre-season and within-season timeframes
  • Review existing technical approaches and procedural mechanisms at regional and national levels for flood and drought forecasting and early warning; prepare a gap and opportunity assessment
  • Assess the potential of integrating information from global data portals into regional data portals to support early warning systems
  • Prepare a briefing document on upgrading existing regional systems and information portals, incorporating good practice from other regions
  • Act as a resource person and support discussions at regional level on adopting improvements, leading to an agreed action plan on early warning
  • Prepare scope, budget, and TOR for implementation of the early warning roadmap under an ensuing loan/grant project; identify possible funding sources including climate funds
  • Facilitate the adoption of immediate priority measures where resources are available

Support to other activities:

  • Provide guidance to other thematic specialists to ensure climate risk assessments are fully integrated into their analysis and recommendations
  • Provide hydrological analysis for screening of candidate projects; identify potential climate risks and propose climate resilience measures
  • Provide hydrology and climate adaptation advice to other activities including strengthening operational procedures of basin water management organizations, water productivity monitoring, water information portal, and water–energy nexus modelling
  • Support discussions of relevant thematic expert groups through provision of experience from other countries
  • Provide inputs to capacity building activities, particularly in-service training on hydrology, climate adaptation, and climate resilience
